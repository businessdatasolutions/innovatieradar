Based on the comprehensive frameworks detailed in your sources, specifically the **Innovation Radar** developed by Sawhney et al. and the **INOVA-tec** contextualization method, I have designed an **Innovation Power Assessment Tool**.  
This tool is structured for you to use directly with clients to diagnose their current innovation strengths, identify neglected opportunities, and benchmark their progress.

### The Innovation Power Assessment Tool

**Overview:**This tool moves beyond the narrow view of innovation as merely "new product development" or "R\&D." Instead, it scores a company across **12 distinct dimensions** of their business system. Innovation is defined here as the creation of substantial new value for customers and the firm by creatively changing one or more of these dimensions 1, 2\.  
**Instructions for the Consultant:**

1. **Assessment:** Interview key stakeholders or survey management using the 12 dimensions below.  
2. **Scoring:** Rate each dimension on a scale of **1 (Low/No Activity)** to **5 (World-Class/Systemic Innovation)**.  
3. **Contextualization:** Apply weighting if specific dimensions are more critical to the client's specific industry (e.g., "Supply Chain" may carry more weight for a logistics firm than a boutique consultancy) 3\.  
4. **Visualization:** Plot the scores on a radar (spider) chart to visualize the client's current "innovation profile" 4, 5\.

#### Part 3: Analysis & Interpretation

**1\. Calculate the Innovation Magnitude**To get a single "Innovation Power" metric, you can calculate the **Magnitude Index** (adapted from INOVA-tec).

* *Formula:* Sum the weighted scores of all dimensions and divide by the number of dimensions 32\.  
* *Interpretation:*  
* **High Performance:** Score \> 4 (Systemic Innovator) 33\.  
* **Average Performance:** Score 3 \- 4 (Occasional Innovator) 33\.  
* **Low Performance:** Score \< 3 (Little or no Innovation) 33\.

**2\. Visualize the Radar**Plot the scores on a radar chart.

* **Round Shape:** Indicates a balanced, systemic innovation strategy (e.g., Apple's iPod touched on Offerings, Platform, Supply Chain, Presence, and Brand) 34\.  
* **Spiked Shape:** Indicates a focused strategy (e.g., a bank focusing heavily on "Presence" and "Brand" but ignoring "Processes") 35\.

**3\. Identify Gaps (The "Blue Ocean")**Use the chart to show clients where their competitors are bunching up. If every competitor is innovating on "Offerings" (product features), advise your client to look at neglected dimensions like "Customer Experience" or "Value Capture" to gain a competitive advantage 36, 37\.
